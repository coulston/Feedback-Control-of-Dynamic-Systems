The fact that when complex numbers multiply, their magnitudes multiply (and similarly for divison) means that we can solve this problem by finding the magnitudes of each term in the expression {\em first} and then multiplying.
\begin{align*}
\left|\frac{(1+j)(2+2j)}{3+4j}\right| &= \frac{\left|1+j\right|\left|2+2j\right|}{\left|3+4j\right|} \\
&= \frac{\sqrt{1^2+1^2}\sqrt{2^2+2^2}}{\sqrt{3^2+4^2}} \\
&= \frac{\sqrt{2}\sqrt{8}{\sqrt{9+16}} \\
&= \frac{\sqrt{16}}{\sqrt{25}}= \frac{4}{5} \\
\end{align*}
